Adolescence Nutrition

Fueling Growth and Development

Get Adolescence Plan

Key Nutrients for Adolescence:

  • Iron: Essential for blood production, energy, and cognitive function. Adolescents, especially girls after menstruation, require increased iron intake. Good sources include lean meats, beans, and fortified cereals.
  • Calcium: Crucial for building strong bones and teeth, critical during peak bone-mass-building years. Dairy products, fortified plant-based milk, and leafy greens are excellent sources.
  • Vitamin D: Aids the body in absorbing calcium, promoting bone health and immune function. Sunlight exposure and fortified foods are important sources.
  • Protein: Necessary for muscle growth, tissue repair, and overall development. Include sources such as lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, beans, and tofu.
  • Zinc: Supports immune function, growth, and wound healing. Found in meat, poultry, seafood, nuts, and seeds.
  • Folate (Vitamin B9): Important for cell division and DNA synthesis, especially critical for female adolescents. Leafy greens, beans, and fortified grains are good sources.
  • Fiber: Promotes digestive health, regulates blood sugar, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Found in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes.

Healthy Eating Habits:

  • Balanced Diet: Ensure a varied intake of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
  • Limit Processed Foods: Reduce consumption of sugary drinks, packaged snacks, and fast foods.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially during physical activity.
  • Regular Meals: Avoid skipping meals, particularly breakfast, to maintain energy levels and prevent overeating later.
  • Mindful Eating: Pay attention to hunger and fullness cues to avoid overeating.
  • Snack Smart: Choose healthy snacks like fruits, yogurt, nuts, or whole-grain crackers.

Tips for Parents:

  • Lead by Example: Model healthy eating habits by making nutritious choices yourself.
  • Provide Nutritious Options: Keep a variety of healthy foods available at home for meals and snacks.
  • Involve Teens in Meal Planning: Include adolescents in meal planning and grocery shopping to promote healthy choices.
  • Encourage Cooking: Teach basic cooking skills to empower teens to prepare their own healthy meals.
  • Limit Screen Time During Meals: Encourage family meals without distractions from phones or TV.
  • Promote Body Positivity: Foster a healthy body image and focus on overall well-being rather than weight.
  • Consult Professionals: Seek guidance from a registered dietitian or healthcare provider for personalized advice.
